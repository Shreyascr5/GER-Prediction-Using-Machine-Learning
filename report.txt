Writing

Principal Component Analysis (PCA) was applied to reduce the dimensionality of the educational dataset prior to clustering. After one-hot encoding, the dataset contained 95 features. PCA analysis showed that 35 principal components were sufficient to retain approximately 95% of the total variance, resulting in a dimensionality reduction of about 63%.

K-Means clustering was then performed on the PCA-transformed data. The Elbow Method and Silhouette Analysis were used to evaluate cluster quality for K values ranging from 2 to 7. The highest silhouette score was obtained for K = 2, indicating that the dataset naturally forms two major clusters. These clusters represent distinct groups of educational characteristics across different state-year observations.
